Abstract

Our project aims to find relationships between students alcohol consumption and different factors by analyzing the dataset. The dataset provides information that allows us to find ways to help students improve their academic performances and reduce their alcohol consumption by analyzing the factors, including various social, economic, academic, and environmental variables, and alcohol consumption ratings.

Keywords

High School Student; Alcohol Consumption; Family background; Educational Levels; Academic Performance

Introduction

For our project, we want to analyze how different factors and high school students alcohol consumption behavior are related to each other. It is important to understand what is influencing the habit of student drinking, and how their drinking behavior will affect their class/academic performance. There are four questions we want to mainly explore with the data:

  1. Which gender has a higher average rating on workday and weekend alcohol consumption?
  2. Does the amount of student alcohol consumption depends on the amount of free time afterschool they have?
  3. How does alcohol consumption impact academic performance among students?
  4. Were there any factors that correlated with lower alcohol consumption levels?

The dataset we choose from Kaggle provides us with data about the general background of the student and their family. It also records students drinking level and their grades over three periods for Math class and Portuguese language class. For our project, we plan to use the data from the Math class dataset for analysis. We want to explore if any factors recorded in the dataset are related to the level of students alcohol consumption. Specifically, we want to examine if there is a relationship between student alcohol consumption and factors like gender, family relationships, and parents education level. And also, we wonder what contributes to the most positive and negative effects on students academic success. Our project aims to find ways to help high school students improve their academic performances and reduce their alcohol consumption.

Related Work

Alcohol consumption among adolescents has become one of the public health problems of global concern. Compared with adults, adolescents are physically and psychologically immature. The consequences of alcohol will not only affect the health of young people, but also easily lead to inappropriate violence or sexual behavior, traffic accidents, interrelationship influence, and academic grade fluctuation. These three articles describe drinking among high school students from the United States, Canada, and northeastern Portugal. They all collected data on the age, gender, academic performance, and frequency and amount of drinking of the study student population. These factors are all connected to the dataset we studied, and we can use the data of these three additional research articles to compare and study their data and results. In the study The effects of alcohol use on academic achievement in high school, Economics of Education Review, researchers examine the relationship between student alcohol consumption and student grade point average (GPA) using data from the U.S. National Longitudinal Study of Adolescent Health. The article found that alcohol consumption had a small negative effect on male students GPAs and no significant effect on female students GPAs. However, alcohol consumption increased school absences and difficulty completing school-related tasks. In another study Identifying patterns of alcohol use among secondary school students in Canada: A multilevel latent class analysis, researchers aim to see if there are some social or family factors associated with the amount and pattern of alcohol consumption among students. The article found a significant association between gender and membership in the alcohol drinking level (nondrinkers to heavy drinkers), with the male sex being associated with increased odds of membership in high-risk drinking groups. Other factors are not as significantly associated with alcohol consumption as the gender factor. We also include the study Pattern of Alcohol Consumption by Young People from North Eastern Portugal to support and cross-analyze our dataset since our dataset collects information from two Portugal schools. This study also classified students drinking behavior and analyze their academic performances. With these three data, we can comprehensively judge whether there are some same phenomena or factors that lead to students drinking behaviors and habits by comparing the conditions of different countries and the included research factors. And also look if there are some supplements or related information that we can use for our projects later progress.

What, if any, ethical questions or questions of power do you need to consider when working with this data?
As our data compares a variety of socio-economic factors with a students chosen behavior, we need to understand the imbalances in resources and support among the participants of the study, and the effect that may have on their chosen behaviors. Additionally, it can be easy to categorize certain behaviors as negative, but as data scientists its important to look at all observations through a non-biased lens.

What are possible limitations or problems with this data? (at least 200 words) This data set deals with a variety of environmental factors that could potentially impact a student and then compares them to their specific patterns and behaviors, including the consumption of alcohol. These factors include everything from their age, sex, address, parents occupations, absences, academic failings, and health. Throughout our project, we must refer to the notion that correlation does not always equal causation. Some of these factors, such as a mother or fathers job, may align with a higher amount of alcohol consumption of a student but not be the actual cause for that consumption amount. We will have to take this into consideration when making conclusions for certain trends that we may observe throughout the project. Additionally, some of our information is self-reported data, and there may be a margin of error where some participants didnt answer realistically or honestly. The participants are school aged young adults who may not want to answer truthfully personal factors such as the amount of alcohol they chose to consume or how often they go out. The most accurate information for each participant may be impossible to ever really know, so we will have to use the data we are given and make our best conclusions to our research questions after evaluating the data.

Implications

Student alcohol consumption has been a controversial social issue. From the dataset we analyze, well be able to see the possible correlations, not necessarily direct cause and effect relationships, between alcohol consumption and many other factors. Then well be able to see if it is alcohol leading to certain behaviors, or it is under certain conditions students consume more alcohol. For example, people can see visually the correlations between alcohol consumption of the students with their family size, presence of both parents, and education levels of parents, etc. By reading the data, well be able to answer questions like What are the most possible factors that cause students to consume alcohol more? and What are the most direct influences brought by the over-consuming of alcohol? Psychologists and policymakers could use such data to help analyze teenagers mental health conditions and to aid mentally or physically.
